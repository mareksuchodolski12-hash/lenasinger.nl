## The Psychology of Live Entertainment

**Creating Emotional Connections**

Corporate environments are often sterile and transactional. Live music breaks through that sterility. It creates human connection—and humans do business with people they feel connected to.

When a professional vocalist performs at your event, attendees experience:

- **Emotional engagement**: Music activates the emotional centers of the brain
- **Stress reduction**: Proven that live music lowers cortisol (stress hormone)
- **Memory enhancement**: Events with emotional moments create longer-lasting memories
- **Conversational ease**: Live music of the right volume and type lubricates social interaction
- **Brand association**: Positive emotional experiences associate with your company brand

**The Difference Between Recorded Audio and Live Performance**

Recorded music (even great recorded music) is static. A live performer responds to the room, reads the energy, and adjusts. A professional vocalist can sense when energy is flagging and increase intensity. They can feel when guests need background music vs. focal-point entertainment.

It's the difference between watching a recorded comedy special and attending a live comedy show. Both have the same jokes, but only one truly engages you in real-time.

## What NOT to Do: Common Corporate Event Music Mistakes

**1. Assuming More Volume = Better Entertainment**

Many event planners default to a DJ blasting dance music at corporate events. This might work for certain occasions, but for most corporate gatherings, it's counterproductive. When your clients can't hear each other, they leave. When your employees are shouting to talk? That's not fun—it's frustrating.

A professional vocalist allows intelligent volume management. Music can be the highlight without overwhelming the event.

**2. Using Only Background Music**

Corporate events need dynamic music program:

- Cocktail hour: Background entertainment level
- Presentations: Atmospheric support, not distraction
- After dinner: Focal entertainment (people sit and listen)
- Later evening: Can increase energy

Static volume throughout = static event experience.

**3. Not Considering Your Audience**

Tech startup party ≠ Law firm client appreciation event ≠ Bank awards ceremony.

The music, presentation style, and overall approach should match your industry culture. A live vocalist who understands this adjusts their song selection and presentation accordingly.

**4. Hiring Entertainment That Hasn't Done Corporate Work**

There's a difference between great nightclub performers and great corporate entertainers. Corporate events have different pacing, different audience engagement patterns, and different objectives.

**5. Forgetting About Audio-Technicalities**

Venue acoustics impact everything. A corporate ballroom has completely different sound dynamics than a restaurant. A good performer brings their own equipment and conducts a sound check beforehand. Poor audio = poor impressions.

## How to Select the Right Live Entertainment for Your Event

**Questions to Ask Potential Performers**

**1. "Do you have experience with corporate events?"**

If they mostly perform weddings or nightclubs, they might not be the right fit. Corporate events have specific needs.

**2. "What's your technical setup?"**

- Do they bring their own equipment or use venue AV?
- Do they do a sound check before guests arrive?
- Can they handle various room layouts?
- Do they have a backup plan if equipment fails?

**3. "What song styles can you perform?"**

You need someone versatile enough to:

- Do sophisticated jazz/acoustic standards for cocktail hour
- Perform current pop hits guests recognize
- Adjust based on your audience's demographics

**4. "Can you customize your set for our event?"**

Every corporate event is different. Can they adapt?

**5. "What's your experience with the acoustic/technical aspects?"**

- Have they performed in your venue before?
- Can they work with your venue's AV team?
- Do they handle their own mixing or does your AV team?

**6. "Tell me about your professional background"**

Training matters. Classical training + contemporary performance experience = polished professional.

**7. "References from corporate clients?"**

Ask for 2-3 corporate event references. Call them. Ask specifically:

- Was the performer professional and reliable?
- Did the entertainment enhance the event?
- Would you hire them again?

## The Entertainment Timeline for Your Event

**60 Minutes Before Event Starts**
Performer arrives, does sound check, adjusts for room acoustics

**Event Start - First 30 Minutes**
Guests arriving, networking area. Live music at moderate volume, engaging but conversational.

**Transition to Dinner**
Music volume decreases as guests move to dining area

**During Dinner (if applicable)**
Soft background music, very low volume if presentations happening

**Post-Dinner Entertainment**
If event includes awards, announcements, or recognition—live music can provide atmosphere

**Later Evening**
Music volume can increase if you want to transition to dancing/celebration

**Packing Up**
Professional performer breaks down quietly without disrupting event wind-down
